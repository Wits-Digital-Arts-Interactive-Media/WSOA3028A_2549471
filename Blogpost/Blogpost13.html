Reflecting on the Internet as an Ethical Space

What Does the Internet as an Ethical Space Mean to Me?

Reflecting on the internet as an ethical space really gets me thinking about how we interact online and how our actions affect others. As a South African digital art student who loves animation and various art media, this idea hits close to home. It’s about creating a digital world where respect, empathy, and responsibility are at the forefront of everything we do.

For me, the internet as an ethical space means a place where I can share my art and animations without worrying about plagiarism or negative comments. It’s about being part of a community that values and respects creativity and diversity. An ethical internet would support artists like me, allowing us to express ourselves freely while also considering the impact of our work on others.

What Would This Look Like?

Inclusive and Respectful Communities

In my ideal ethical internet, online communities for artists, animators, and gamers would be inclusive and supportive. We’d have platforms where everyone’s work is respected, feedback is constructive, and harassment is nonexistent. It would be a place where different cultures and artistic styles are celebrated, not ridiculed.

Responsible Content Creation and Sharing

Being responsible online means giving proper credit to creators and thinking about how our work affects others. For me, this involves making sure my animations are culturally sensitive and contribute positively to the community. It’s about creating art that’s mindful of different perspectives and experiences.

What Are the Challenges We Face?

Anonymity and Accountability

One major challenge is the anonymity of the internet, which can lead to people behaving badly because they don’t have to face consequences. This anonymity can result in cyberbullying, trolling, and the spread of false information, making the internet a less safe space for everyone.

Digital Divide

There’s also the issue of the digital divide. Not everyone has the same access to the internet or digital literacy skills. This can create inequalities in who gets to participate and be heard online. In South Africa, and in many other places, this divide can marginalize certain voices, making it harder for everyone to benefit from the internet equally.

Intellectual Property Concerns

As an artist, protecting my work online is a constant worry. Digital art can be easily copied and shared without permission, which undermines our rights as creators. We need better systems to protect our work while still encouraging sharing and collaboration.

CSS Implementation Process

Initial Challenges and Failures

Implementing the CSS to reflect my style guide has been a mix of successes and learning experiences. Initially, I struggled with ensuring that the typography, particularly the Canva Sans Bold for headings, appeared correctly across all devices. There were issues with the font size not scaling properly, causing the headlines to either look too large on mobile devices or too small on larger screens. Additionally, achieving the right balance with the colour palette was challenging. The primary colour, #004AAD (Royal Blue), didn’t contrast well against the black background initially, making some elements hard to read. Adjusting the shades and adding subtle shadows helped mitigate this issue.

Ongoing Work

Currently, I am focusing on refining the grid layout to ensure consistency and visual harmony. Aligning elements and spacing them evenly has required tweaking and testing across different browsers to maintain the responsive design I aim for. I’m also enhancing the interactive elements, such as buttons and hyperlinks, by adding hover effects and animations that align with my aesthetic of vibrant sophistication. Ensuring that the high-resolution images load efficiently without compromising the site’s performance is another area I am diligently working on, using responsive images and optimizing file sizes. Overall, while there have been setbacks, each challenge has brought me closer to creating a visually stunning and immersive digital experience as envisioned in my style guide.
