Documentation of Interaction Design (IxD) Process:

Goal Alignment:

In the initial stages of my Interaction Design (IxD) process, I meticulously aligned the goals of my website with my personal brand identity and the specific needs and expectations of my target audience. Through comprehensive research and analysis, I identified key objectives such as creating an immersive user experience, showcasing my skills and expertise, and fostering engagement and interaction with my content. These goals served as guiding principles throughout the design and development phases, ensuring that every design decision was purposeful and aimed at achieving the desired outcomes.

Information Structure - Content Mapping:

To establish a coherent and intuitive information structure for my website, I conducted a thorough content mapping exercise. This involved organizing and categorizing the various types of content I wanted to include, such as portfolio projects, blog posts, essays, and design concepts. I carefully considered the hierarchy and relationship between different content elements, ensuring that users could easily navigate between them and find the information they were looking for. By mapping out the flow of information from the homepage to specific sections, I aimed to create a seamless and engaging user experience that encourages exploration and discovery.

User Flow - Screens, Behaviour, and Decisions:

Understanding user behavior and decision-making processes was integral to designing effective user flows for my website. Through user research, persona development, and usability testing, I gained insights into how my target audience interacts with digital interfaces and the decisions they make along the way. This informed the design of screens and interfaces that guide users through the website, anticipating their needs and providing clear pathways to relevant content. By considering factors such as navigation, interaction design, and content layout, I aimed to create user flows that are intuitive, efficient, and conducive to achieving the website's goals.

List and Explanation of Selected Interface Elements:

  • Navigation Menus: Clear and intuitive navigation menus provide users with easy access to different sections of the website, allowing them to explore content effortlessly.
  • Calls-to-Action (CTAs): Thoughtfully placed CTAs prompt users to take specific actions, such as subscribing to a newsletter, exploring portfolio projects, or reading blog posts, encouraging interaction and engagement.
  • Interactive Elements: Interactive elements such as sliders, carousels, and accordions add dynamic functionality to the website, enabling users to interact with content in a more engaging and immersive manner.
  • Visual Hierarchy: By leveraging visual hierarchy techniques such as typography, color, and spacing, I prioritized and emphasized important content elements, guiding users' attention and facilitating information comprehension.
  • Responsive Design: Ensuring that the website is fully responsive across various devices and screen sizes is essential for providing a consistent and seamless user experience, regardless of the user's chosen platform.

Through the careful selection and integration of these interface elements, I aimed to create a user-centric design that effectively communicates my brand message and facilitates meaningful interactions with my audience.
